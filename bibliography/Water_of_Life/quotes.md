# The Water of Life — Selected Quotes

Extracted from *The Water of Life: A Treatise on Urine Therapy* by John W.
Armstrong (Ebury Publishing, 2011).

## The Healing Power of Urine

> "Urine, on being taken into the body, is filtered; it becomes purer and purer
> even in the course of one day's living upon it, plus tap-water, if required.
> First, it cleanses, then frees from obstruction and finally rebuilds the vital
> organs and passages after they have been wasted by the ravages of disease. In
> fact it rebuilds not only the lungs, pancreas, liver, brain, heart, etc., but
> also repairs the linings of brain and bowel and other linings, as has been
> demonstrated in the case of many 'killing' diseases, such as consumption of
> the intestines and the worst form of colitis. In fine, it accomplishes what
> fasting merely on water or fruit juices can never achieve."
> — Chapter III

> "The most pregnant of all facts is the outstanding fact that urine, however
> thick, concentrated, scanty and seemingly 'poisonous' it may appear at the
> onset of such diseases as genuine Bright's disease, influenza and others,
> very soon becomes filtered and greatly increased in volume when freely
> imbibed."
> — Chapter III

> "Urine is not dead matter, but so to say, flesh, blood and vital tissues in
> living solution."
> — Chapter V

> "It is not a specific for any given disease, it is a specific for health."
> — Chapter XX

> "The only 'food' for sick people is urine, seeing that among its other
> functions it replaces tissue in a manner nothing else can do."
> — Chapter VII

> "Not only have I never seen a failure with the urine-fast plus tap water
> therapy (all the urine passed should be drunk) but the fall of temperature
> has taken place within any time from 36 to 72 hours, followed by complete
> recovery within a few days."
> — Chapter X

> "As already mentioned, the more frequently it is taken the more innocuous
> does it become."
> — Chapter III

> "Urine is the skin food par excellence as also the remedy for every kind of
> skin disease."
> — Chapter VII

## Historical and Scriptural References

> "There came a moment when, feeling very weak and ill, I recalled the text in
> Proverbs V. which runs, 'Drink waters out of thine own cistern.'"
> — Chapter IV

> "'... when thou fastest, anoint thy head and wash thy face ...' (Matthew VI,
> 17.) I am fully aware that the interpretation I put on this injunction may
> seem far-fetched, but as I have said, it none the less gave me an idea, and
> having read it, proceeded to rub my head, face, neck and other parts of my
> body with urine, and the palpitations ceased."
> — Chapter XVII

> "An universal and excellent remedy for all distempers inward and outward —
> Drink your own water in the morning nine days together and it cures the
> scurvy, makes the body lightsome and cheerful. It is good against the dropsy
> and jaundice ... Wash your eyes with your own water and it cures sore eyes
> and clears and strengthens the sight ... Wash any green wound with it and it
> is an extraordinary good thing."
> — *One Thousand Notable Things* (early 19th century), quoted in Chapter II

> "The volatile salts of urine — It powerfully absorbs acids and destroys the
> very root of most diseases in human bodies ... purifies the whole mass of
> Blood and Humors ... cures Rheumatism and Hypochrondriac diseases, and is
> given with admirable success in Epilepsies, Vertigoes, Apoplexies,
> Convulsions, Lythargies, Migraine, Palsies, Lameness, Numbness, loss of the
> use of limbs, atrophies ... and most cold and moist diseases of the head,
> brain, nerves, joints and womb."
> — *Salmon's English Physician* (1695), quoted in Chapter II

> "The wiser of the ancient Greeks used nothing but urine for the treatment of
> wounds. The Eskimos even to this day adopt the same measures."
> — Chapter II

> "The Llamas of Thibet and the yogis with whom he associated prior to his
> attempt, claim to live to a great age by means of the use of urine. By the
> same means they can also traverse deserts inaccessible to ordinary mortals."
> — Chapter II (on Maurice Wilson, who attempted to climb Everest)

## Armstrong's Self-Cure

> "I fasted for forty-five days on nothing but urine and tap water — and this,
> despite the doctor's assertion that eleven days without food was the limit to
> which a human being could go."
> — Chapter IV

> "At the end of this treatment I felt and was 'an entirely new man.' I weighed
> 140 lbs., was full of vim, looked about eleven years younger than I actually
> was, and had a skin like a young girl's."
> — Chapter IV

> "By dint of drinking every drop of water that I pass, living on a
> well-balanced diet, and never eating more food per diem than I consider the
> body requires, I look and feel much younger than most men of my age, and keep
> free from those major and minor ailments to which the body is said to be
> heir."
> — Chapter IV

> "By dint of rubbing with urine, I have found it possible to fast — if not too
> ill to start with — without interrupting one's daily tasks ... not even a
> doctor with his impressive instruments can detect from my heart that I am
> abstaining from food."
> — Chapter XVII

## Fasting Combined with Urine Therapy

> "The policy of forcing sick people to eat 'in order to keep up their
> strength' is in my opinion responsible for thousands of untimely deaths. Food
> cannot be assimilated by a sick body which is already full of obstructive
> matter."
> — Chapter VII

> "To an invalid whose instincts and organs rebel against taking nourishment,
> food acts like a poison."
> — Chapter XIV

> "On no account should enemata be resorted to during a urine-fast, for Nature
> must be left to determine when the bowels shall move. What we have to
> remember is that in fasting, urine, taken via the mouth heals, rebuilds and
> re-conditions the vital organs including the intestines, and while this
> process is taking place, often the bowels seem, as it were, to go to sleep
> and relapse into a state of inactivity, which in severe cases may even last
> as long as 19 days."
> — Chapter XXI

> "What I would like to emphasise is that not once until I began to live on a
> urine-fast plus tap water did I observe the growth of new skin in cases which
> had been regarded as entirely hopeless."
> — Chapter V

> "The longest fast I can record was that of a man who fasted 101 days for
> blindness brought about by a sting in one eye and the long continued use of
> atropin in both."
> — Chapter XVIII

## Remarkable Case Histories

> "By the eighteenth day of the fast the foot was quite normal; the urine had
> formed new skin, and there was no trace whatever of the livid abrasions. The
> foot had healed without even leaving a scar."
> — Chapter V (gangrenous foot)

> "In a fortnight there was no sign of the gangrene, and new skin had grown in
> place of the cavities. The diseased leg, which previously had become twice
> the size of the other one, was now completely normal — not even a scar
> remaining anywhere to remind her of what she had suffered!"
> — Chapter V (leg gangrene, amputation refused)

> "Lady of 62; diagnosed cancer of the bowel. Colotomy advised by the
> profession, but refused. Was under 6 stone and rapidly wasting away. Cured
> in three weeks. At the date of writing is 84."
> — Chapter VI

> "Lady of 40. In 1935 developed the type of growth sometimes called 'rope
> cancer.' Surgeons pressed for immediate excision but offered no hope of a
> permanent cure ... Cured by the urine-fast, etc., in twenty-three days. Is
> not only still well, but looks young and beautiful."
> — Chapter VI

> "Mrs. R. (1923) ... Fasted on urine and drank 2 pints of plain cold
> tap-water daily. Her husband rubbed her from head to foot with his own urine
> for two hours a day, and packs wrung out in urine were placed over both
> breasts day and night. Cure in ten days."
> — Chapter VI

> "Man of 60, after two years' constant medical supervision and treatment for
> heart, developed Bright's disease. Finally, given up by his two doctors,
> specialist was called in ... Specialist said the case was hopeless. Nothing
> more to be done. I undertook the case. Patient passed 40 pints of water five
> days later, and returned to business in six weeks — cured."
> — Chapter VII

> "Case of paralysis, premature old age, loss of memory, etc. Male patient,
> aged 60. Medical verdict, 'a few weeks to live.' ... Memory and his speech
> returned in 20 days during the first fast ... This same man who had lost his
> hair, not only regained it during the second fast — his head was rubbed
> daily — but instead of being grey it assumed its original colour."
> — Chapter XIV

> "Diseased Kidney. Mr. G.D. In May, 1944, he was in hospital for the removal
> of his right kidney ... Mr. D. refused the operation and came to me. He took
> to urine drinking quite naturally, drank all he passed, fasted repeatedly for
> several days at a time, and within a few weeks the condition was improved to
> the extent that there was no pain and the urine was of normal colour. In
> three months Mr. D. returned to the hospital and was told that there was
> nothing wrong with his kidney."
> — Chapter XIV (reported by O. Warnock-Fielden)

## Arguments Against Conventional Medicine

> "How comes it that for over fifty years the orthodox cancer researchers have
> been occupying themselves with the cause and cure of malignancy, yet still
> can suggest nothing better than the knife, radium or X-rays?"
> — Chapter I

> "'Disease ... becomes a vested interest, and consciously or unconsciously,
> the doctors foster it as such. It is quite a common observation that doctors
> produce disease. Moreover, the whole system and philosophy of our dealing
> with disease is mistaken.'"
> — Dr. W. H. White, quoted in the Foreword

> "Far from being an exact science, in spite of all 'scientific' tests that
> patients are subjected to in these days, it still remains such an inexact
> science that ten different doctors have been known to give ten different
> diagnoses relative to so apparently simple an ailment as persistent
> headaches."
> — Chapter III

> "They are so full of erudition that 'they cannot see the wood of truth for
> the trees of learning'! That is one very cogent reason why sooner or later
> they reject the simple remedy or treatment for the complex, no matter how
> efficacious that simple remedy has proved to be."
> — Chapter III

> "'I have examined women with what would in orthodox care have resulted in the
> removal of one or both breasts. These happy mortals have declined my advice,
> gone under urine-therapy and returned to my consulting rooms without even a
> scar to suggest the healing of incurable malignancy ... It is a sad
> reflection that my profession thrives on disease and the inhuman propaganda
> of official scaremongering.'"
> — Dr. Rabagliati, quoted in Chapter VI

> "Cancer itself was child's play to deal with in comparison to the
> after-effects of radium, and had I offered any hope of curing these I should
> have been a quack imposter of the most bare-faced type."
> — Chapter VI

> "A real quack in the worst sense of the word is the man who professes to cure
> what he knows he cannot cure."
> — Chapter VI

## The Body's Self-Healing Capacity

> "Within Man himself is to be found the substance to cure his diseases,
> whether they be so-called wasting diseases or otherwise."
> — Chapter I

> "Nature is a far more efficient Healer than are so-termed scientific methods
> which involve mutilation."
> — Chapter VI

> "The name of the disease is of merely academic interest, and has nothing to
> do with the curing of it."
> — Chapter VIII

> "When doctors try to bring down a patient's temperature by unnatural means,
> they are frustrating Nature and may be endangering the patient's life. A
> fever is really a curative process on the part of Nature to burn up certain
> toxins in the body."
> — Chapter X

> "There is no such thing as a local disease; there are only localised
> symptoms. Cleanse the body of its impurities by a fast on urine and plain
> water, and pyorrhoea vanishes automatically."
> — Chapter XIV

> "'When man first appeared upon the earth, some provision must have been made
> for his security against extinction ... Would the vital importance of his
> freedom from disease be neglected? If what is stated in these pages is
> correct, the means whereby health may be attained is always close at hand,
> easy to carry out, marvellous in its simplicity and free to everyone.'"
> — O. Warnock-Fielden, quoted in Chapter XIV

> "'What cannot be cured by the forces of the body cannot be cured by the
> forces outside the body.'"
> — Dr. T. Wilson Deachman, quoted in Chapter II
