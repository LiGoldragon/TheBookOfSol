# On Water, Minerals, and the Error of Micronutrition

What survives fire describes death. Ash is the residue of combustion, the catalogue of what remains when life has gone.

What are called "minerals" are rocks: inert residues identified by combustion. They are catalogued by burning living matter and then credited with sustaining life. The procedure is a category error. Combustion reveals what remains when life is gone; it tells nothing about what life recognizes, accepts, or uses.

A living companion and a corpse are distinguished by presence, not origin. The cold body once lived; it does not warm or respond now. The same clarity applies to nourishment.

Pure water is always superior to mineralized water. Mineral loading forces filtration and expulsion because it presents itself as foreign residue. Water unburdened by additions hydrates by absence. When water carries rocks, the body treats it as something to be removed.

Coconut water restores because it is a living plant plasma. The ash-table of "minerals" is irrelevant to its action. Reduce coconut water to ash and nothing restorative remains. To drink the ashes would be absurd. Yet modern dietetics reasons precisely this way: by fire, not by life.

## On the Mineral Myth

The mineral myth arises from confusing what can be weighed after destruction with what functions during life. What persists after incineration is countable; what sustains vitality is not. Nourishment then gets treated as an accounting problem, entries debited and credited on a ledger compiled from ruins.

Classical medicine never made this mistake.

> *Āhāraḥ prāṇakaraḥ*\
> "Nourishment is that which carries life."\
> — *Caraka Saṃhitā*

Life is the criterion. What does not carry life cannot be its support.

> *Dravyāṇāṁ guṇāḥ karma ca*\
> "Substances are known by their qualities and actions."\
> — *Caraka Saṃhitā*

Rocks have qualities and actions, but no nourishing ones. Their action is burden, filtration, expulsion.

## Micronutrition as an Accounting Fiction

So-called "micronutrition" treats nourishment as bookkeeping: trace remnants tallied, balanced, and optimized as though vitality obeyed ledgers derived from ash. The living system is reduced to a warehouse, intake to inventory, health to compliance with columns of figures.

Life is recognized, not administered.

The living system does not consult tables compiled from destruction. It responds to coherence, familiarity, and living continuity.

Direct recognition has always been the standard.

> *Na hi jñānena sadṛśaṁ pavitram iha vidyate*\
> "Nothing purifies like direct knowledge."\
> — *Bhagavad Gītā* 4.38

## Plasma Recycling as Resolution

Plasma recycling (*amarolī*) dissolves the confusion at the root. The fluid is already recognized because it is already part of the living continuum. No sourcing problems, no mineral mythology, no electrolyte performance.

> *Yukta āhāra-vihārasya*\
> "Stability is preserved through right intake and restraint."\
> — *Bhagavad Gītā* 6.17

Conservation precedes restoration. Loss precedes collapse.

Pure water when water is needed. Living fluids when they exist. Ash belongs to fire.

Plasma returned to life remains sovereign.
