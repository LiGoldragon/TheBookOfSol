# Death, the Sorcerers' Option, and the Eternal Essence — Extracts from Armando Torres

Primary-source extractions from Armando Torres's two books — *Encounters with the Nagual: Conversations with Carlos Castaneda* (First Light Press, 2002/2004) and *The Secret of the Plumed Serpent: Further Conversations with Carlos Castaneda* (Hade, 2014) — on the triple thesis at issue here: physical immortality is impossible and was the specific trap that ensnared the old seers; the body's death is necessary and irrevocable; and incarnate life is the unique window in which awareness may be trained to survive its own container.

Torres was a Mexican apprentice who attended the 1990s Mexico-City workshops Castaneda ran in the years just before his death, and later travelled in Mexico to meet living members of Toltec-descended healer lineages. Both books reconstruct conversations that Castaneda's published books cover only obliquely, and they give the death-doctrine of the don-Juan line in plainer speech than Castaneda himself permitted on the page.

## I. Death as irrevocable reality

Torres frames the opening of the death-chapter in *Encounters* with the account of how Castaneda undercut his own youthful assumption of unlimited continuity — the assumption that lies behind all second-hand beliefs about immortality.

> "Over the years, the need to understand the world had led me to store a lot of scientific or religious explanations on almost everything, which all had one common denominator: A great trust in the continuity of man. By helping me to see the universe with the eyes of a sorcerer, Carlos destroyed that sensation in me. He made me see that death is an irrevocable reality, and that to avoid acknowledging it by applying second-hand beliefs is shameful."\
> — Armando Torres, *Encounters with the Nagual*, "Awareness of Death"

## II. The Castanedan refusal of immortality

The core of Castaneda's workshop teaching, as Torres records it, is that belief in personal immortality is itself the enemy. Once the belief is removed, the real opportunity can be seen.

> "If you want to know yourselves, be aware of your personal death. It's not negotiable, it is the only thing that you can seriously own. Everything else may fail, but not death, you can take that as a fact. Learn how to use it to produce real effects in your lives."\
> — Armando Torres, *Encounters with the Nagual*, "Awareness of Death" (Castaneda, lecture voice)

> "Also, stop believing in fairy tales. Nobody needs you out there. None of us is so important that it justifies inventing something as fantastic as immortality. A humble sorcerer knows that his destiny is the same as that of any other living being on Earth. So, instead of having false hopes, he works concretely and with great effort to escape the human condition, and to reach the only exit we have: the breaking of our perceptual barrier."\
> — Armando Torres, *Encounters with the Nagual*, "Awareness of Death" (Castaneda)

The workshop instruction on the next step is the reduction of whatever can be reduced — the "baggage" — because nothing of that baggage is coming with us. The body itself is part of what cannot be carried.

> "Once aligned with death, you will be able to take the next step: reducing your baggage to a minimum. This is a prison world, and we must leave it as fugitives; we can't take anything with us. Human beings are travelers by nature. To fly and to know other horizons is our destiny. Do you take your bed or your dining table with you on a trip? Synthesize your life!"\
> — Armando Torres, *Encounters with the Nagual*, "Awareness of Death" (Castaneda)

## III. Death as the magical event, not the natural one

Torres records Castaneda's insistence that death is *not* an ordinary event governed by ordinary laws. It is personal, singular, and therefore an act of power — the one act on which the whole of a life converges.

> "Thanks to their frequent excursions into the unknown, they know that death is not natural; it is magical. Natural things are subject to laws, but death is not. To die is always a personal event, and for that sole reason, it is an act of power."\
> — Armando Torres, *Encounters with the Nagual*, "Awareness of Death" (Castaneda)

> "Death is the gateway to infinity. A door made to the exact measure of each of us, which we will all pass through someday, returning to our origin. Our lack of understanding impels us to see it as a common reducer. But no, there is nothing common about it."\
> — Armando Torres, *Encounters with the Nagual*, "Awareness of Death" (Castaneda)

> "Destiny's coach will take all of us, without distinction. But there are two kinds of travelers: warriors who can leave with the totality of themselves, because they have fine-tuned every detail of their lives, and ordinary people, with boring existences, without creativity, whose only hope is in the repetition of their stereotypes until the end."\
> — Armando Torres, *Encounters with the Nagual*, "Awareness of Death" (Castaneda)

## IV. The old seers' attempted immortality — and why it is not immortality

In *The Secret of the Plumed Serpent* Torres gives, in more technical form, the don-Juan account of the ancient Toltec seers who tried to defeat death by transforming themselves into inorganic beings. He presses Castaneda on the question the ordinary reader most wants to ask: is this immortality? Castaneda's answer is the same as the Hermetic answer: no. It is a *suspension* of death, not a cancellation; and the price of the suspension is a form of slavery to the inorganic realm.

> "Like life, death touches us all but it is not the same for all. Through their investigations, sorcerers discovered ways of extending the duration of individual awareness to its maximum."\
> "Does that mean that they became immortal?"\
> "Not at all. What I'm saying is that sorcerers managed to find a way of suspending death, not of cancelling it. For them, to die does not have the same meaning it has for ordinary people. For them, the opposite of dying is not immortality, but continued existence."\
> — Armando Torres, *The Secret of the Plumed Serpent*, "The Sorcerers' Option" (Castaneda)

> "The trick lies in understanding that to be alive does not necessarily mean being an organism; rather, it means being aware."\
> — Armando Torres, *The Secret of the Plumed Serpent*, "The Sorcerers' Option" (Castaneda)

The sorcerers-seers who undertook this project knew that biological life is finite but that the *energy* that animates it is not. The project was to train the individual bundle of that energy to be aware of itself. This is the whole of the Castanedan offer: not the preservation of the body, but the formation, during life, of a self-aware bundle.

> "Next, the sorcerers observed that, although biological life lasted a limited period of time after which it ceased to exist, the energy that animated matter was eternal. They saw that it was possible, by means of discipline and self-determination, to train that bundle of energy that forms us to become aware of itself. Joining their two discoveries together, those sorcerers-seers proposed to remain aware forever. That is what they referred to when they spoke of attaining freedom: what they were really trying to do was prolong the individuality of awareness as much as they could."\
> — Armando Torres, *The Secret of the Plumed Serpent*, "The Sorcerers' Option" (Castaneda)

> "Theirs is a phenomenal battle, with the flame of awareness bravely and unceasingly struggling to stay alight. Indeed it is as it has been said, the light of a candle against the glare of a billion stars."\
> — Armando Torres, *The Secret of the Plumed Serpent*, "The Sorcerers' Option" (Castaneda)

## V. The mechanism of the exit: the body is left behind

Torres records Castaneda's description of the various exit strategies sorcerous lineages have devised. The strategies differ in their mechanics; they agree on one point: the physical body is not what leaves. Don Juan's own lineage, Torres notes, left in an especially clean form: "they did not leave even their ashes behind; they left with everything and their sandals." The idiom is poetic; the meaning is precise. What left was not the organism. The organism was consumed, completely, in the exit.

> "There are sorcerers who have transformed themselves into trees or have buried themselves alive to defy death; other groups believe that body is an obstacle, which is why they free themselves of their physical part at the moment of leaving. Don Juan and his party did not leave even their ashes behind; they left with everything and their sandals. In a most elegant way, they dissolved into thin air. Warriors of total freedom, on the other hand, merge with the emanations at large and disappear forever, consumed by fire from within. They are never heard of again; theirs is truly a journey of no return."\
> — Armando Torres, *The Secret of the Plumed Serpent*, "The Ultimate Manoeuvre" (Castaneda)

The book is cold-eyed about the uncertainty at the far end:

> "Some of them believe that it is still possible to preserve the sense of individuality in that condition. Others think that, instead of being total freedom, merging with emanations at large means total death and nothing more. No one knows what really happens."\
> — Armando Torres, *The Secret of the Plumed Serpent*, "The Ultimate Manoeuvre" (Castaneda)

## VI. The true essence is the luminous body, not the flesh

In *The Secret of the Plumed Serpent* Torres records a teaching from don Melchor, one of the healers he trained with, that makes the luminous-body / physical-body distinction economical:

> "Just as fire needs firewood as fuel in order to exist, the luminous body, which is our true essence, uses the physical body as the primary source of energy. Hence, the better one tunes one's organism, the more one can do at the energy level."\
> — Armando Torres, *The Secret of the Plumed Serpent*, "Becoming a Healers' Assistant" (don Melchor)

The body is firewood. It is not the fire.

## VII. Life is the fleeting event — death is what gives it meaning

Torres records the same don Melchor, in the same setting, giving the plainest single-sentence formulation of the whole teaching:

> "Life is a fleeting event in the eternal flow of energy. Death is what gives it meaning. Modern man's confusion comes from thinking that organic life and awareness are one and the same. But that is a mistake, caused by lack of experience of dreaming."\
> — Armando Torres, *The Secret of the Plumed Serpent*, "Becoming a Healers' Assistant" (don Melchor)

> "We are all going to die, and there is no other way. It's only a question of time, so you are already dead: what more is there for you to lose? If we look at things that way, the world is our oyster."\
> — Armando Torres, *The Secret of the Plumed Serpent* (don Melchor)

> "Death is real; it is a process of initiation. You must always bear in mind that you are already dead. … There are only two types of people: those who consider themselves immortal and those who know that they are going to die. Do not get distracted, dare to look at your imminent end! The awakening is now or never."\
> — Armando Torres, *The Secret of the Plumed Serpent* (don Melchor)

## VIII. Cross-reference note

Torres's editorial eye makes one of the connections this article is built around explicit: he treats don Juan's group's disappearance "with everything and their sandals" as the Toltec version of the same pattern the *Corpus Hermeticum* describes as the body being "released" at the first zone. Both traditions say: what crosses does not cross wearing flesh. The forging happens in life. The passage happens through death. The physical body is not, ever, the thing being saved.
