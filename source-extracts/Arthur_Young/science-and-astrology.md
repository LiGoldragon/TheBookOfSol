# Arthur M. Young — *Science and Astrology* — Extracts

Arthur M. Young (1905–1995) was the inventor of the Bell helicopter and the founder of the Institute for the Study of Consciousness in Berkeley. His *Reflexive Universe* (1976), *Geometry of Meaning* (1976), and the 1987 broadside *Science and Astrology — The Relationship Between the Measure Formulae and the Zodiac* are sustained attempts to give formal scientific status to life, evolution, and consciousness. The 1987 essay is his most compact statement of the central thesis, and the one this extract draws from.

The thesis. Science and astrology share a common foundation in the division of the circle. The **measure formulae** of physics and the **signs of the zodiac** are two complete and internally consistent vocabularies for the same set of distinctions. Both proceed by dividing wholeness — the quantum of action — into combinations of three parameters (mass, length, time) using the same angular partitioning. The two systems face in different directions but stand on the same ground.

> "Science & Astrology is not an attempt to define or explain the zodiac or the measure formulae but is an examination of the common foundation shared by both, an examination that holds regardless of the use or abuse of either. Of course, in our day and age, astrology wears the disguise of the bawdy courtesan, but behind this masquerade lies the flesh and bones of first principles. While founded on intuitive insight, these principles can be shown to correlate to those on which the measure formulae — the time-tested vocabulary of physical science — is based."\
> — Arthur M. Young, *Science and Astrology*, jacket copy

The extract follows Young's own section order. The aim is to bring forward the load-bearing claims in his own words.

## I. The conflict is ideological, not factual

Young opens by refusing the framing that pits the two as rivals on the same question. The geocentric/heliocentric correction has nothing to do with astrology, whose measure depends only on angle. The real conflict is between two ideologies — determinism and the recognition of self-initiated action — and Newton's calculus made determinism *possible* but never *necessary*.

> "To the modern mind astrology is dismissed as superstition, a delusion of the prescientific age before it was found that planets go around the sun rather than the sun and planets around the earth, and that the earth rotates and the celestial sphere does not. Strictly speaking, it does not matter to astrology which goes around which, since astrological measure depends only on angle, but the earlier geocentric view had become associated with astrology and when the geocentric view was proved false, astrology dropped out of fashion."\
> — Arthur M. Young, *Science and Astrology*

> "Newton's laws made possible a new ideology, determinism, an ideology that became the basis for almost all science. I say that Newton's laws made possible the ideology of determinism. They did not require it, since it could still be the case that such laws had limits of applicability."\
> — Arthur M. Young, *Science and Astrology*

The bird in the window is Young's irreducible counter-example. The stone falls predictably; the bird flies away. The two outcomes obey the same molecular chemistry, but only one is explained by the law that explains the other.

> "If we drop a stone out of a window it will fall at a predictable rate... But what about a bird? If we drop a bird out a window, it doesn't drop — it flies away."\
> — Arthur M. Young, *Science and Astrology*

> "Science doesn't recognize the full implication of self initiated action, and even good philosophers who reject determinism do not have a proper scientific explanation of how a bird flies. Scientists are too busy with what they can explain to be concerned with what they cannot, much the same way that many astrologers are too preoccupied with what works rather than with why or how it works."\
> — Arthur M. Young, *Science and Astrology*

Young's diagnosis of ideology is the cleanest line in the essay:

> "The failure to explain life is not the failure of an ideology; it is not the purpose of ideology to explain. An ideology is rather a promise to explain."\
> — Arthur M. Young, *Science and Astrology*

The two central dogmas, set against each other:

> "The central dogma of science is that the same experiment, when repeated at a different time, will yield the same result. The central dogma of astrology is that different experiments made at the same time (i.e., simultaneous births) will yield the same result. Science searches for general laws and principles and provides a map of what are essentially spatial relationships. Even if time is included it is treated as space-like and symmetrical. On the other hand, astrology deals with itineraries unique to individuals, rather than with a general map. Astrology reduces all measure to time and timing, and endeavors to predict the time and, to some extent, the significance of events to one person."\
> — Arthur M. Young, *Science and Astrology*

Science deals with time as linear, astrology with time as cyclic. The systems are antithetical in direction but share the same foundation — the angular division of the circle.

## II. The third derivative — control as the mark of life

Newton's calculus gave three measure formulae for the motion of inert bodies: position (L), velocity (L/T), and acceleration (L/T²). Multiplied by mass, three more: moment (ML), momentum (ML/T), and force (ML/T²). Multiplied again by L, three more: moment of inertia (ML²), action (ML²/T), and energy (ML²/T²) — with power (ML²/T³) the only third-derivative in the textbook bottom row.

Young then notices what the textbooks omit:

> "Notice that there is only one third derivative, power, in the bottom row. But are there other third derivatives to fill in this row? This is the case: there are two more, L/T³ and ML/T³. The first, L/T³, is change of acceleration, or **control**. It is by pushing the accelerator pedal, applying the brakes, or steering (which is also a change of acceleration) that we control the car. The second is control times mass — mass control — the difference between control of a canoe and the control of an oil tanker."\
> — Arthur M. Young, *Science and Astrology*

The omission of these two formulae from the textbooks is not an oversight but a methodological choice: to derive deterministic laws, energy must be held constant — which forbids the third derivative.

> "The omission of control and mass control from scientific textbooks can be accounted for on the grounds that they are not used to find the laws of nature. To find such laws we must not add or subtract energy from the system. This has led to the situation in which 'pure' science omits any reference to these derivatives. It is left for engineers to make use of them, as is done in aeronautics, where the third derivative of position, L/T³, is called 'jerk' and that of momentum is called 'power control.' Neither term is well chosen."\
> — Arthur M. Young, *Science and Astrology*

Restoring the third derivative restores life:

> "By assigning formal status to control we can achieve a true 'science' of life. Such a science — one which recognizes that the third derivative is just as valid as the first and second which made possible the science of the motion of inert bodies — becomes a science of living bodies, and is no longer purely deterministic. The third derivative, or control, makes possible the use of determinism to attain ends. When we drive an automobile we use both determinism and control in order to reach our goal. Without determinism the car would not respond to control; without control we could not use determinism. Both are necessary. It is not a question of determinism versus free will; it is a question of learning the laws of determinism so we can use them to attain our goals."\
> — Arthur M. Young, *Science and Astrology*

## III. The cardinal cross — the learning cycle

The four cardinal signs map to the four steps of the cycle by which a control is acquired. Push the door; it doesn't open; see the sign that reads "Pull"; pull and go in. Four phases: blind action, reaction, observation, appropriate action.

> "These four phases in the learning cycle correspond to Aries, blind action; Cancer, reaction; Libra, observation; and Capricorn, the appropriate action, or control."\
> — Arthur M. Young, *Science and Astrology*

| Phase | Sign | Measure | Symbol |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1. Initial action | Aries (the ram) | Acceleration (L/T²) | charging with head down — blind outrush at the spring equinox |
| 2. Reaction | Cancer (the crab) | Velocity (L/T) | sideways motion — natural reaction to onrush |
| 3. Observation | Libra (the scales) | Position (L) | the harvest — measure of what was planted |
| 4. Control | Capricorn (the goat) | Control (L/T³) | sure-footed on the mountain — conscious action at the solstice |

The cycle reverses the order of the derivatives — L/T², L/T, L, L/T³ — because *integration* is the natural order of events: a body starts (acceleration), gains velocity, reaches position, and from there is controlled toward a new destination. Integration was the move Galileo used before calculus existed.

> "The learning cycle describes the acquisition of control. Once control is learned and we can perform an action correctly it becomes instinctive or unconscious. In every case, however, the factors involved include the third derivative, L/T³ — change of acceleration, or control. While the third derivative is obvious in the case of a car, it is equally applicable to the control we exercise over our own bodies. We learn control from infancy — to crawl, to walk or use our vocal cords. Animals do the same, and plants control their metabolism. In fact it is through control of nature's laws that life becomes possible."\
> — Arthur M. Young, *Science and Astrology*

Astrology's anticipation of this is structural, not accidental. Of the four cardinal signs, three correspond to first-and-second-derivative measures, but the fourth, Capricorn, is *only* the third derivative. The third derivative was waiting in the zodiac before science noticed it was missing from its own table.

> "As a matter of fact, I did not read the third derivative into astrology; rather, it was astrology that suggested to me that I should look for the third derivative in science, where it is not generally recognized or assigned a function."\
> — Arthur M. Young, *Science and Astrology*

## IV. Placement — the dimensional angles

The whole circle is 360°. Time is given 90°, because four divisions by time return to the start (T⁴ = 1). Mass is given 120°, because four signs of the same element are 90° apart and three modalities of the same element are 120° apart. Length is given 30°, because LT = M (30° + 90° = 120°) and the four 30°-steps of length must close the gaps inside each element.

> "With these assignments of angle to the parameters, T = 90 degrees, M = 120 degrees, and L = 30 degrees, and the further convention that multiplication ( = addition because we are dealing in exponents) is counterclockwise, and division ( = subtraction) is clockwise, we can move about freely and still keep our bearings."\
> — Arthur M. Young, *Science and Astrology*

> "Strictly speaking, there is nothing in the placement of the measure formulae around a circle that was not implicit in their nature. What I have done is to find the ultimate dimension of each formula and express it as an angle; but I would not have thought of the idea if I had not first encountered it in astrology."\
> — Arthur M. Young, *Science and Astrology*

## V. The twelvefold correspondence

The full table — Young's central correlation:

| Element | Mutable (relationship / stimulus) | Cardinal (action / response) | Fixed (state / result) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Fire** *(noumenal, projective)* | **Sagittarius** — Quantum of Action (ML²/T) — *Hunch, Intuition* | **Aries** — Acceleration (L/T²) — *Blind Action* | **Leo** — Force (ML/T²) — *Being* |
| **Water** *(phenomenal, projective)* | **Pisces** — Moment of Inertia (ML²) — *Belief* | **Cancer** — Velocity (L/T) — *Change* | **Scorpio** — Momentum (ML/T) — *Transformation* |
| **Air** *(noumenal, objective)* | **Gemini** — Power (ML²/T³) — *Knowledge* | **Libra** — Position (L) — *Observation* | **Aquarius** — Moment (ML) — *Significance* |
| **Earth** *(phenomenal, objective)* | **Virgo** — Energy (ML²/T²) — *Work, Fact* | **Capricorn** — Control (L/T³) — *Control* | **Taurus** — Mass Control (ML/T³) — *Establishment* |

Three binary distinctions cut across the table:

> "Air and Earth are objective; Fire and Water are projective.\
> Fire and Air are noumenal; Water and Earth are phenomenal.\
> Water and Air are general; Fire and Earth are particular."\
> — Arthur M. Young, *Science and Astrology*

The triad — mutable, cardinal, fixed — corresponds to **past, present, future**, or **stimulus, response, result**. This gives the asymmetry of time a formal status, which physics, busy with time-reversible equations, has been unable to grant.

## VI. The fixed signs — embodiment

Mass is the operator that converts action into state. Acceleration becomes force, velocity becomes momentum, position becomes moment, control becomes mass-control. In astrology's vocabulary, action becomes embodiment: blind action becomes *being*, change becomes *transformation*, observation becomes *significance*, control becomes *establishment*.

> "This mass, then, whatever it be, transforms acceleration into force, gives it embodiment, as it were. Correlation with astrology translates this as Aries, blind outrush, transformed into Leo, or being. Leo is the sign ruled by the sun, and is thought to represent the sun because the lion's mane suggests the sun's radiation."\
> — Arthur M. Young, *Science and Astrology*

The word "fixed" carries the photographer's sense — image first in the developer (change), then in the fixing solution to make change permanent. Momentum *fixes* velocity into transformation; the blacksmith's hammer at thirty feet per second forges the hot iron into a sword.

> "Momentum, mass times velocity, leads to transformation. This is the result or fruit of change. It seems odd that a fixed sign should deal with change, but the word fixed here has the same meaning it has in the process of developing photographic film... In strictly scientific terms, we have the production of force by the momentum of a hammer."\
> — Arthur M. Young, *Science and Astrology*

The four-stroke engine completes the picture. The sequence of intake, compression, explosion, exhaust runs counter-clockwise through the four elements — air, earth, fire, water — in the natural integrative direction.

> "Intake of air (Air) / Compression (Earth) / Explosion (Fire) / Exhaust (Water)... The natural order is that of the signs, of the seasons, and it corresponds to integration. It is also that of the learning cycle. The opposite order is application."\
> — Arthur M. Young, *Science and Astrology*

The connection between *mass* and the older word *Mass* — the rite of transubstantiation — is, for Young, no accident. Max Jammer's *Concepts of Mass in Classical and Modern Physics* traces the term back through Einstein and Newton to the Holy Communion. Mass is what makes a thing what it is.

## VII. The mutables — relationship and stimulus

Each mutable formula carries an L² term — an area, the geometry of relationship.

> "Mutables are relationships, and since it requires an area to express relationship, as in a map or drawing, it is significant that the measure formulae containing L² correlate to the mutable signs. Thus the Quantum of Action, ML²/T, depends on the area of the electron orbit as distinct from its radius. In the formula for Moment of Inertia, ML², one L is tangential and the other radial, again suggesting area."\
> — Arthur M. Young, *Science and Astrology*

The four mutables are the four stimuli that initiate the four kinds of action — and the four kinds of relationship between the self and the object.

> "First Row: Hunch or intuition is the 'stimulus' for blind action, and results in pure being, play. In physics the quantum of action is first cause; it is responsible for all exchanges between atoms, molecules, and perhaps cells, as well as for the creation of particle pairs (proton and antiproton). It is also responsible for all our sensations — sight, taste, and so on."\
> — Arthur M. Young, *Science and Astrology*

> "Second Row: Faith (Pisces) is that which keeps us going in the absence of other support. In physics this is Inertia, defined as the tendency to remain in a given state of rest or motion. When multiplied (operated upon) by change it leads to transformation. The element here is water, that is, emotions, which are not an end in themselves but can lead to transformation."\
> — Arthur M. Young, *Science and Astrology*

> "Third Row: Knowledge or data (Gemini) is the relation between facts. In physics this corresponds to Power ('Knowledge is power'). Gemini the twins, or similarity of relationship, leads to theories and laws, ultimately to significance. The repeatable experiment is of major importance in science; but the data have to be observed (Libra), and the purpose of observation is to discover significance (Aquarius)."\
> — Arthur M. Young, *Science and Astrology*

> "Fourth Row: Energy, which is equivalent to work, is necessary to obtain facts. The word fact comes from facio, to do. Facts are the antecedent to control. For example, when the doctor makes a diagnosis he draws on the particular facts which apply to his patient — lab tests, symptoms, and so forth. On the basis of these facts he prescribes the treatment (control) and this results in the cure."\
> — Arthur M. Young, *Science and Astrology*

(Note Young's footnote here: the medical prescription symbol *Rx* originally meant *I have read your astrological chart*, a usage also known as a *radix*.)

The mutables also map cleanly onto Hegel's thesis-antithesis-synthesis, with a fourth term — analysis — added on the opposite axis:

> "The issue is not so much whether the thesis or the antithesis is correct; both are beliefs. But their synthesis will depend on finding out the facts. This requires analysis. Pisces, the two fishes tied together and pulling different ways, typifies beliefs — two coming out of two. Virgo, the virgin birth, is one out of one — an emphasis on the particularity of facts, their pertinence in matters of dispute. Gemini is relationship; as the twins it is two out of one (mother) as against the opposite sign Sagittarius, the centaur shooting an arrow. The centaur has the body of a horse and the torso of a man, so the arrow is one coming out of two, i.e., synthesis."\
> — Arthur M. Young, *Science and Astrology*

The four kinds of relationship the mutables name:

- **Gemini** — the relation of a thing to itself (definition, concept, the theoretical ideal).
- **Virgo** — the relation between particular facts about a particular thing.
- **Pisces** — the relation of value the self projects onto the object (like, dislike, beauty).
- **Sagittarius** — the relation of purpose, why the object exists, Aristotle's final cause.

> "A chair is something to sit on. While not inherent in the object, it is nonetheless the purpose of any man-made object that brought it into existence — Aristotle's final cause."\
> — Arthur M. Young, *Science and Astrology*

## VIII. Force, the photon, and the projective

The middle of the essay turns on the question of what is *objective*. Science insists that what it studies is objective — out there, measurable. Young presses the point: a force is never measured directly; it is measured by comparison with another force. The spring scale's deflection is itself a force. Forces are felt, not seen.

> "It is the comparison that is objective, not the force itself. Earlier I mentioned Eddington's comment that force is an 'elusive concept.' I would suggest that it is not a concept at all. Concepts are objective; we communicate them intellectually. Forces are felt; we must experience them."\
> — Arthur M. Young, *Science and Astrology*

The argument escalates with the photon. The photon — the quantum of action — cannot be observed twice; its observation is its annihilation.

> "Science has discovered an entity that belongs in this category — the quantum of action, or photon. This is the light we see by; it is also the unit of action that causes all chemical and physical changes of state. Photons are single units of action. They may contain any amount of energy, but the greater the energy the shorter their duration or period, so that the product of their energy times the associated time is always the same, a constant — Planck's quantum of action."\
> — Arthur M. Young, *Science and Astrology*

> "It is provocative, exciting in fact, to realize that science has discovered this 'atom' of action to be the most fundamental entity of the universe — a universe which until recently was supposed to be based on material particles."\
> — Arthur M. Young, *Science and Astrology*

> "The photon is not objective because it cannot be observed more than once. If one person sees it no one else can; in fact, its observation is its annihilation... We are dealing with what Zen philosophy is all about: the photon is not something seen, it is the act of seeing itself, and yet it is thanks to science that we know its nature."\
> — Arthur M. Young, *Science and Astrology*

The quantum of action is the verb in the present tense — initiative, decision, the act before the object. The map of the universe is woefully incomplete if it admits only objects.

> "It is a central dogma of science that the world is exclusively objective. But the present moment is not only not objective, it is the only thing that is; whatever else we might suppose to be real or important. It is our present activity with regard to the world, even if this be passive observation, that is important. We are part of the world, a window by which it views itself."\
> — Arthur M. Young, *Science and Astrology*

> "The quantum of action — alias uncertainty — for which a more appropriate term might be **initiative**."\
> — Arthur M. Young, *Science and Astrology*

## IX. Wholeness — the quantum of action as the cosmic atom

Harlow Shapley, near the end of his life, wrote that the three parameters of science — mass, length, time — were inadequate; they gave no hint of life, consciousness, or the drive of evolution. A fourth parameter, he urged, was needed. Young's answer: the fourth parameter is the *whole* the other three divide.

> "I had myself looked for this extra parameter for years before I recognized that it was already in science but had been overlooked. It is the whole of which mass, length and time are divisions."\
> — Arthur M. Young, *Science and Astrology*

> "We thus assign the whole circle to action, and so the divisions of action are divisions of the circle."\
> — Arthur M. Young, *Science and Astrology*

By the dimensional assignments (T = 90°, M = 120°, L = 30°), energy itself has angular measure zero:

> "Energy = ML²/T² = 120° + 2 × 30° − 2 × 90° = 0°"\
> — Arthur M. Young, *Science and Astrology*

Action — the quantum, the photon — has measure 90°, which Young reads as *timing* — the positive aspect of uncertainty, the choice of *when*.

> "Maximum uncertainty is thus the largest possible angle, 360 degrees or 2π."\
> — Arthur M. Young, *Science and Astrology*

The quantum of action is unconstrained because it is the whole the constraints are divisions of. The constituent atom of the universe is not the material particle but the act:

> "Action is one of the formulae, [so] it is on the periphery, but it is also the origin of the rest and hence the center of the circle, which is actually not a circle but the cross section of a torus whose origin is also on the periphery."\
> — Arthur M. Young, *Science and Astrology*

> "The material atom does not truly conform to the definition of atom as an 'indivisible unit.' The photon does comply with this requirement, so by this criterion too it is the atom in the sense of the ultimate constituent."\
> — Arthur M. Young, *Science and Astrology*

## X. The hypersphere and the seven-fold arc

Eddington's unification of relativity and quantum theory: the volume of ordinary space is (4/3)πR³; adding uncertainty multiplies by 2π; adding Eddington's "limitation of scale" multiplies by 3/4. The result:

> "3/4 × 2π × 4/3 πR³ = 2π²R³ — which is the formula for the Einstein-Eddington hypersphere. It is also the formula for the torus with an infinitely small hole, which I have dealt with in *The Reflexive Universe*."\
> — Arthur M. Young, *Science and Astrology*

> "Einstein was unable to unify relativity and quantum theory because he was looking for a 'field.' A field is two or more dimensional, whereas the unification contributed by Eddington is at a more fundamental level — that of rotation, or zero dimensionality."\
> — Arthur M. Young, *Science and Astrology*

The 3/4 is the load-bearing detail. The cycle does not close at four; it turns back at the fourth position (control, Capricorn) and takes three more steps — application — before returning to the start.

> "Recall that this is the point in the learning cycle that corresponds to control. If we were to go on to 1 again we would start a new cycle. If we are to stop, or rather if we are not to go past 4, we must turn back, which reverses the direction of rotation and converts the learning cycle to a cycle of application."\
> — Arthur M. Young, *Science and Astrology*

The pattern is seven-fold: four steps forward (learning), three steps back (application). The example Young uses is tennis — learning to grip, swing, hit, and place the ball, then playing the game with these motions trained into the unconscious.

> "All other skills have this 'anatomy,' involving seven distinct steps, which begin and end with spontaneous action."\
> — Arthur M. Young, *Science and Astrology*

The four-color theorem applies to the sphere; the torus requires seven. The arithmetic of map-coloring confirms the arithmetic of the cycle.

> "The so-called coloring problem confirms this sevenness. To color a map on the surface of a sphere requires no more than four colors (the four-color problem); but the torus, which is the shape of the figure whose formula is 2π²R³, requires seven colors."\
> — Arthur M. Young, *Science and Astrology*

## XI. Time, cycle, and meaning

Cyclic time is not the monotonous repetition modern thought has rejected. The day-and-night cycle, the seasons, youth-and-age — these repetitions are not the same each time; they accumulate. Repetition is also a *spiral*, in which the smaller cycle is encompassed by the larger.

> "Cyclic time in fact makes it possible for time to integrate and to be significant."\
> — Arthur M. Young, *Science and Astrology*

Meaning itself, Young argues, is angular.

> "It is possible to define meaning as arising from angle — 180 degrees being opposition, 90 degrees being mediation or meaning, and so forth. But this is still a static description. The richer meaning is provided by sequence, as in the learning cycle, which occurs in time."\
> — Arthur M. Young, *Science and Astrology*

Science treats its laws as timeless, and is uncomfortable with entropy (which goes down) and evolution (which goes up). Astrology can fall into the opposite trap, equating prognostication to fate. Both errors come from misreading time.

## XII. Closing — freedom as first cause

The essay's last paragraph is the cleanest statement of Young's position. Both sciences and astrologies can become idolatries — the idols being determinism and fate. Both idolatries proceed by omitting *first cause*, which is freedom, which is the quantum of action, which is the centaur Sagittarius shooting his arrow.

> "Perhaps the true message of spiritual teachings is not that we should worship, but that we should not worship idols. Both science and astrology generate idols — science can idolize determinism, astrology can idolize fate — but such idolatry in both cases rests on the omission of first cause which is our freedom — represented in the measure formulae as the quantum of action and in the zodiac by Sagittarius."\
> — Arthur M. Young, *Science and Astrology*

> "With the acknowledgment of the third derivatives, including action which can be thought of as the third derivative of energy, we have, if not the basis for the science of life, at least an invitation to live."\
> — Arthur M. Young, *Science and Astrology*
