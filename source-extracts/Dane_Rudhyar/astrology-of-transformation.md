# Dane Rudhyar — *The Astrology of Transformation* (1980) — Extracts

*A Multilevel Approach.* Wheaton, IL: Theosophical Publishing House / Quest Books, 1980.

Written when Rudhyar was 85, the book is what he himself calls his **astrological testament**. It is the late synthesis of forty-five years of work — completing the arc that began with *The Astrology of Personality* (1936), continued through the humanistic phase to *Person-Centered Astrology* (1972), and entered the transpersonal phase with *From Humanistic to Transpersonal Astrology* (1975) and *The Sun is Also a Star* (1975). *Transformation* gathers it all and applies it through the structural innovation that gives the book its subtitle: **a multilevel approach** — four levels at which both human existence and astrological interpretation can be understood, and the *transitions* between them.

## I. Astrology as testament — the prologue declaration

> "This book could be called my astrological testament, in the sense that it brings to a conclusion my attempt to reformulate and give a new direction to modern astrology."\
> — Dane Rudhyar, *The Astrology of Transformation*, Prologue

The prologue traces the work as two long phases — *humanistic* (1933–68) and *transpersonal* (1975–80) — and names the term whose meaning the book settles:

> "I began to use the term **transpersonal** in 1930, long before the movement of transpersonal psychology was started, and with a meaning quite different from the one the word has recently taken on in the field of psychology. I defined as transpersonal a process of '**descent**' of transcendent spiritual power and illumination through the normal consciousness, and eventually through the whole personality of a human being."\
> — Dane Rudhyar, *The Astrology of Transformation*, Prologue

> "In traditional religious terms, as a man prays to God his soul reaches up to the Divine; and God answers by an outpouring of 'grace' — a descent of the Holy Spirit. The transpersonal approach I have been presenting does not follow any strictly religious system of thought; neither is it 'mystical' in the usual sense of the term. It is essentially metaphysical and cosmological — or one may say **cosmontological**, as it refers to cosmic 'being' (*ontos*)."\
> — Dane Rudhyar, *The Astrology of Transformation*, Prologue

## II. Holarchy — beyond holism

Rudhyar coins the term *holarchy* to extend Jan Smuts's *holism* into a *hierarchy of organic wholes*. Every whole is a part of a greater whole; every whole has parts that are themselves wholes.

> "The only basis I can find practical and effective as well as philosophically — and even aesthetically — significant is a multilevel approach to the human being. In terms of cosmology, or 'cosmontology', this approach leads one to consider the universe as a **hierarchy of fields of existence or systems of organization**. It leads to the concept, not merely of 'holism' in the sense Jan Smuts used it in his seminal book, *Holism and Evolution*, but of what I have called **holarchy**."\
> — Dane Rudhyar, *The Astrology of Transformation*, Prologue

The book's most condensed statement of the transpersonal posture:

> "The whole acts not only **in** the individual, but **through** the individual. The whole realizes itself in and through the acts, feelings, and thoughts of its individualized participants who have become open to its descents of power. As this occurs, the transindividual state of existence is reached."\
> — Dane Rudhyar, *The Astrology of Transformation*, Prologue

> "Humanity is only a part of vaster wholes — the planet, the solar system, our galaxy — and these wholes hierarchically set the cosmic and planetary stages; yet, on the stage of the Earth's biosphere Man is a crucially important performer. Humanity no doubt has a role to perform, at least broadly defined by its place within these vaster wholes. The 'score' is not of Man's own making, but the performance is nevertheless his, for better or for worse."\
> — Dane Rudhyar, *The Astrology of Transformation*, Prologue

## III. Chapter One — the two basic ways of meeting life's confrontations

The opening chapter contrasts two modes of human response to life's challenges. The first is the *biological-instinctive* mode — flowing with the change as a part of nature. The second is the *separate-conscious* mode — taking a stand against the change as an *I* with continuity:

> "When human beings live at a purely biological, instinctual, animal-like level of existence, their reaction when faced with potentially harmful and/or painful situations is to adjust as smoothly as possible to what is happening, opposing a minimum of emotional resistance to natural events, flowing with the tide of change which they trust will once more bring favorable conditions. They do not feel separate from nature and its tidal and seasonal movements."\
> — Dane Rudhyar, *The Astrology of Transformation*, Chapter One

> "Man, however, has within himself the capacity somehow to separate himself from the flow of events and ever-changing life-situations; he becomes aware that they happen to him — a 'him' that has a degree of objectivity and permanence within the flux of unceasing natural changes."\
> — Dane Rudhyar, *The Astrology of Transformation*, Chapter One

Each mode has its corresponding civilization, its corresponding philosophy, its corresponding astrology. The book's task is to show that **both modes operate at every level of human development**, and that the practitioner must learn to read both.

## IV. Chapter Three — the four levels of human functioning

The book's structural backbone is the four-level scheme. Each level is a way of being human; each entails a corresponding way of doing astrology. The astrologer's first task on meeting a client is to *intuitively sense* at which level the client is centred, and toward which level the client is moving.

### Level 1 — Biological

The organism. Breathing, blood, food, reproduction, security. Synthesizing all biological needs is the *need for security* — not just personal but, more deeply, *species-level*.

> "Synthesizing, as it were, all basic biological needs is the need for security, not only as a particular person, but more deeply still as a member of the human species; for, at the biological level, the preservation and expansion of the species as a whole is actually of greater concern — unconscious though it may be — than the safety of any particular body belonging to that species."\
> — Dane Rudhyar, *The Astrology of Transformation*, Chapter Three

### Level 2 — Sociocultural

Becoming a *person* — a participant in a community. Culture, religion, language, taboo. The collective psyche.

> "No human being should be called a person unless he or she has become a functional participant in a social collectivity... a culture is really far more than a manner of living. Culture establishes itself within the collective psyche of a community as a power that controls the basic emotional responses to everyday living and the collective mentality (primitive and instinctual or more formally developed as it may be) of all the human beings born within the social organism."\
> — Dane Rudhyar, *The Astrology of Transformation*, Chapter Three

### Level 3 — Individual

The autonomous individual. The ego at its full development. The Renaissance, the Enlightenment, modern individualism. Rights, uniqueness, originality. Most contemporary astrology — humanistic astrology included — addresses this level.

> "When a human being, having become a sociocultural person, is actually able to operate (or at least consciously and emotionally claims to be able to operate) as a truly autonomous individual regardless of sex, color, race, class, or religion, new needs take form in this individual's consciousness; and they have to be met. These needs are particular to each individual; yet, they take specific kinds of forms for various groups or classes of individuals."\
> — Dane Rudhyar, *The Astrology of Transformation*, Chapter Three

### Level 4 — Transpersonal

The descent of more-than-individual power into the individuated personality. The Path of Initiation in modern language.

> "On the foundation represented by the individual level, a fourth level is possible: the transpersonal level. And the word transpersonal, as I use it, does not merely refer to what is *beyond* the personal. It implies a 'descent' of power that meets, as it were, the aspirations or 'ascent' of the human person who, having concentrated a long series of efforts toward the goal of actually becoming a free and autonomous individual, eager to stress his or her uniqueness and originality, has found these efforts leading to serious crises of belief and identity."\
> — Dane Rudhyar, *The Astrology of Transformation*, Chapter Three

The four levels are not exclusive; they are stages of a development. Most modern people operate primarily at Level 2 (sociocultural) modified by Level 1 (biological) needs, with intermittent breakthroughs to Level 3 (individual). The transpersonal level (Level 4) is a *possibility* — for the contemporary minority that has fully constituted itself as individuals and reached the crisis through which the descent of trans-individual power becomes possible.

## V. A multilevel astrology — same data, four interpretations

The same birth-chart can be read at any of the four levels. The level at which one reads it depends on the level at which the client is operating.

> "I shall now try to explain how astrology can take a different form and quality at each of these levels, and how astrologers operating at each of these levels should deal with the main data provided by the cyclic interplay of celestial motions."\
> — Dane Rudhyar, *The Astrology of Transformation*, Chapter Three

> "Any horoscope is susceptible of interpretation at all possible levels. It may be the chart of a cat or a horse, the incorporation of a business firm, or a horary inquiry. And a particular person, while born into a family or social situation conditioning his or her operating at a particular level, may pass through a more or less prolonged and/or successful process of transition leading from that level to a succeeding one (or ones)."\
> — Dane Rudhyar, *The Astrology of Transformation*, Chapter Three

The astrologer's craft now becomes the craft of *level-recognition* — sensing where the client is, and offering interpretation pitched at that level rather than at a generic mid-level.

## VI. The eight chapters

The book's full structure:

1. **The Two Basic Ways of Meeting Life's Confrontations** — the two modes (flow vs. stand), the cultures they produce, the astrologies they require.
2. **Astrology as a Symbolic Language** — astrology as the great mythos of cosmic order; the role of symbols in a desacralised civilization.
3. **Four Levels of Interpreting Human Experience and Astrological Data** — the load-bearing chapter, summarised above.
4. **The Biological Level** — astrology as nature-cycle and species-cycle.
5. **The Sociocultural Level** — astrology as the language of cultural roles, social moments, and karma.
6. **The Individual Level** — humanistic astrology in its proper place: the person realising himself or herself as an individual.
7. **The Transpersonal Level** — astrology as the language of the descent of trans-individual power; the galactic frame; the trans-Saturnian planets as the doors to the path.
8. **Progressions and Transits** — re-read at all four levels; the birth-chart as seed-image, progressions as the inner unfoldment, transits as the dialogue with the world.

An Epilogue closes the book.

## VII. The trans-Saturnian planets and the galactic dimension

The transpersonal level is technically inseparable from the **trans-Saturnian planets** — Uranus, Neptune, Pluto — and from the **galactic frame** that *The Sun is Also a Star* developed. Saturn marks the boundary of the personal-individual ego; the planets beyond Saturn name the three doors through which the trans-individual descent reaches the individuated person.

In the multilevel interpretation, the same trans-Saturnian planet means *different things at different levels*. Uranus at the biological level is mutation; at the sociocultural level, social-revolutionary upheaval; at the individual level, the lightning of original insight; at the transpersonal level, the lightning of *initiation* itself. The astrologer's craft includes knowing which face Uranus is showing in a given chart at a given moment of a given life.

The galactic frame extends this: the Sun is not the centre of the universe but one star in the galaxy; the individual is not the centre of his world but one node in the planetary humanity; the transpersonal level is the conscious recognition of that node-status.

## VIII. Progressions and transits at the transpersonal level

The closing chapter on progressions and transits re-reads the basic predictive techniques through the multilevel lens. The birth-chart at all levels is *the seed-image of the unfolding personality*. Progressions are the *inner* rhythm of unfoldment; transits, the *outer* dialogue with the world.

> "If we consider a birth-chart the symbolic seed of an individual personality, we can readily see that the growth of the germinating seed into a large tree depends, on the one hand, on the inner rhythm of the natural process of unfoldment of seed-potentialities into the actualized form of the tree, and on the other hand, upon weather conditions, sunshine and rain, and what insects, large animals, and human beings might do to the growing tree."\
> — Dane Rudhyar, *The Astrology of Transformation*, Chapter Eight

> "At the individual level of interpretation, transits and progressions have much the same meaning as the one I will now explain in relation to a transpersonal interpretation. But at the individual level, the birth-chart is the dominant factor, because a strictly individual life should lead, in principle, to the fulfillment of what was potential in the natal chart. In **transpersonal astrology**, on the other hand, the dominant concern of the astrologer is to understand the **dynamism and rhythm of the transformative process itself**. The birth-chart is only the starting point of this process, which even physical death may not end — if the individual has succeeded in raising his or her I-center to the transindividual (or symbolically, the 'galactic') level."\
> — Dane Rudhyar, *The Astrology of Transformation*, Chapter Eight

> "From the transpersonal point of view, '**rebirth**' may be more significant than '**birth**', if the karma of the past has been lived through, absorbed and neutralized."\
> — Dane Rudhyar, *The Astrology of Transformation*, Chapter Eight

## IX. The book's place in Rudhyar's arc

*The Astrology of Transformation* completes a five-decade movement:

- *The Astrology of Personality* (1936) — the foundation: astrology as the language of personality-integration; the chart as algebra of life.
- *The Pulse of Life* (1943) — the dynamic-zodiac thesis: the twelve signs as twelve phases of the year-cycle of the personalising-and-collectivising forces.
- *An Astrological Study of Psychological Complexes* (1948) — the four-urge structure mapped onto the planetary pairs; Saturn-Moon, Jupiter-Mercury, Mars-Venus, Uranus-Neptune-Pluto.
- *The Practice of Astrology* (1968) — the thirteen-step practitioner's manual; the astrologer as *astrotherapist*.
- *The Astrological Houses* (1972) — the houses as space-fields of individual experience; the three-level birth.
- *The Astrology of Transformation* (1980) — the synthesis: the four levels of human functioning and the multilevel astrology that meets each of them.

Together they constitute the canonical body of **humanistic and transpersonal astrology** as Rudhyar built it.

## X. Closing — Man as performer on a stage not of his making

The prologue's closing image:

> "Humanity is only a part of vaster wholes — the planet, the solar system, our galaxy — and these wholes hierarchically set the cosmic and planetary stages; yet, on the stage of the Earth's biosphere Man is a crucially important performer. Humanity no doubt has a role to perform, at least broadly defined by its place within these vaster wholes. The 'score' is not of Man's own making, but the performance is nevertheless his, for better or for worse; and every truly individualized human being is a responsible aspect of Humanity-as-a-whole."\
> — Dane Rudhyar, *The Astrology of Transformation*, Prologue
