# Raw Veganism and Fruitarianism — The Harm in the Clinical Record

A verified compilation of the modern clinical and forensic literature on what happens to people who live on raw and fruit-dominant diets. The classical corpus already named the mechanism: fruit (*phala*) is *auṣadha* (medicine) before it is *āhāra* (food), the staple of the body is *anna* (cooked grain), and a diet of one *rasa* (taste) eaten in chronic excess is debilitating before any specific food is named (see [*Fruit as Medicine*](./Fruit_as_Medicine.md) and [*Apathya* and *Mitāhāra*](./Apathya.md)). This file gathers the empirical record of that depletion — the cohort studies, the forensic cases, the named complications — so the diet articles can stand a clinical witness beside the textual one.

Every citation here was checked against the primary publication (PubMed record or the journal of record), not recalled from secondary summary. Where a study qualifies its own finding, the qualification is carried with it. The supporting articles are [*Yogis Don't Eat Fruit*](../diet/Yogis_Dont_Eat_Fruit.md), [*Fruitarianism*](../diet/Fruitarianism.md), and [*Vegan*](../diet/Vegan.md). Verification notes on the citations those articles currently carry — three misattributed classical verses and one unsupported causal claim — live in the poet report `reports/poet/86-raw-vegan-fruitarian-harm-citation-deepdive-2026-06-12.md`.

A word on vocabulary. The project names nourishment in Āyurvedic terms and avoids the reductionist micronutrient register in its doctrinal prose. A compilation of clinical evidence is the one place the modern terms must appear verbatim, because the finding being cited is stated in them — a study reports "vitamin B-12," "bone mineral density," "amenorrhea," and the citation is only honest if it uses the published word. Each finding is therefore given twice: once in the language the researcher measured it, and once in the language that named the mechanism first.

## I. The Giessen Raw Food Study — the largest cohort ever assembled

The most substantial body of evidence comes from a single German cohort. Between the mid-1990s and 2005, researchers at Justus-Liebig University in Giessen surveyed and examined several hundred long-term raw-food dieters — the largest such group ever studied. Three of their findings bear directly on the doctrine that fruit and raw plants clear without sustaining.

### Weight loss, underweight, and amenorrhea

> "The consumption of a raw food diet is associated with a high loss of body weight. An average weight loss of 9.9 kg in men and 12 kg in women was observed since the beginning of the raw food diet. Body mass index was below the normal range in 14.7% of the men and 25.0% of the women. About 30% of the women under 45 years of age had partial to complete amenorrhea; subjects eating high amounts of raw food (more than 90%) were affected more frequently. A very strict raw food diet cannot be recommended on a long-term basis."\
> — C. Koebnick, C. Strassner, I. Hoffmann, C. Leitzmann, "Consequences of a Long-Term Raw Food Diet on Body Weight and Menstruation," *Annals of Nutrition and Metabolism* 1999;43(2):69–79

The classical reading is exact. Sustained weight loss to below the normal range is *dhātu-kṣaya* — the wasting of the tissues — and the cessation of menstruation is *ārtava-kṣaya*, the failure of the body to spare anything for *rasa* and *rakta* (plasma and blood) to build the reproductive tissue from. A body kept on the clearing principle alone reaches the floor described in [*Fruitarianism*](../diet/Fruitarianism.md): "as internal fluids thin and lubrication is lost, the same dispersion that clarified now destabilizes." The dose-response — the strictest raw eaters losing the most, menstruating the least — is the *atiyoga* (overuse) curve drawn in human subjects.

### Vitamin B-12 depletion and the unfinished transformation

> "Vitamin B-12 status was deficient in 38% of the raw food dieters. The consumption of a strict raw food diet lowered serum HDL cholesterol (low in 46% of subjects) and raised plasma total homocysteine concentrations, the latter due to vitamin B-12 deficiency. The subgroup using vitamin B-12 supplements regularly had higher plasma vitamin B-12 and lower homocysteine than nonusers."\
> — C. Koebnick, A. L. Garcia, P. C. Dagnelie, C. Strassner, et al., "Long-Term Consumption of a Raw Food Diet Is Associated with Favorable Serum LDL Cholesterol and Triglycerides but Also with Elevated Plasma Homocysteine and Low Serum HDL Cholesterol in Humans," *Journal of Nutrition* 2005;135(10):2372–2378

Raised homocysteine is the blood marker of a transformation the body could not finish — methylation arrested for want of the one factor a plant-only diet cannot supply. In the classical frame this is the signature of *agni* meeting input it cannot fully cook: what is half-transformed accumulates as *āma*, the sticky residue named throughout the corpus as the seed of disease. The study's own escape hatch confirms the diagnosis from the other side — the deficiency lifts only when the missing factor is supplied from outside the raw-vegetal plate, which is to say the plate by itself does not complete the work.

### Dental erosion

> "As part of a larger dietary study, 130 subjects whose raw food intake was more than 95% were examined and compared with 76 controls. Only 2.3% of the raw food group were free of erosive defects, against 13.2% of controls; 60.5% of raw food subjects had at least one tooth with severe erosion, against 31.6% of controls. The raw food dieters consumed a median of 4.8 servings of citrus fruit daily and 9.5 kg of fruit weekly. A raw food diet bears an increased risk of dental erosion compared to conventional nutrition (p ≤ 0.001)."\
> — C. Ganss, M. Schlechtriemen, J. Klimek, "Dental Erosions in Subjects Living on a Raw Food Diet," *Caries Research* 1999;33(1):74–80

The teeth keep the most literal record. *Amla-rasa* — the sour taste — taken in *atiyoga* corrodes the hard tissues directly; the enamel dissolved by 4.8 daily servings of citrus is the visible end of the dispersing, scouring action the *Bhagavad Gītā* assigns to the *rājasic* register (*amla*, *uṣṇa*, *tīkṣṇa* — sour, hot, sharp; BhG 17.9). The mouth shows in mineral what the rest of the body shows in *dhātu-kṣaya*.

## II. Bone mass — the finding and its caveat

A smaller American study examined the skeletons of long-term raw vegans and found them light. The finding is real and is reported here with the qualification its authors insisted on.

> "The 18 raw-food vegetarians (mean age, duration 3.6 years) had significantly lower bone mineral density at the lumbar spine and hip than matched controls on a standard diet. However, markers of bone turnover were normal, serum vitamin D was higher than in controls, and inflammatory markers and leptin were low — suggesting that, despite the low bone mass, bone quality may be preserved rather than osteoporotic."\
> — L. Fontana, J. L. Shew, J. O. Holloszy, D. T. Villareal, "Low Bone Mass in Subjects on a Long-Term Raw Vegetarian Diet," *Archives of Internal Medicine* 2005;165(6):684–689

The study proves low bone mass; it does not prove fracture or frank osteoporosis, and the article that cites it should not claim more than the authors did. What it does corroborate is *asthi-dhātu-kṣaya*, the thinning of the bone tissue, which the tradition reads as a *vāta* condition — the bone is the seat governed by the dry, light, rarefying *doṣa* that a raw, cold, dispersing diet aggravates. The honest statement is the bounded one: the raw vegan skeleton carries less bone, by a diet whose qualities are precisely those Āyurveda names as bone-depleting, and the long-term consequence remains the open question the authors left open.

## III. Vitamin B-12 deficiency across the vegetal-only diets

The Giessen B-12 finding is not an artifact of one cohort. A review of the literature pooled the prevalence across vegetarian and vegan populations and found the deficiency endemic, worst where the diet is strictest and longest.

> "Deficiency was found in 62% of pregnant women, between 25% and 86% of children, 21–41% of adolescents, and 11–90% of the elderly studied. Higher rates were reported among vegans than among vegetarians, and among those who had followed the diet since birth than among those who adopted it later. Vegetarians should take measures to ensure adequate intake of this vitamin."\
> — R. Pawlak, S. J. Parrott, S. Raj, D. Cullum-Dugan, D. Lucus, "How Prevalent Is Vitamin B-12 Deficiency Among Vegetarians?," *Nutrition Reviews* 2013;71(2):110–117

The gradient is the doctrine. The deficiency deepens with the strictness of the exclusion and with the years on it, and it is gravest at the two ends of life where the tissue is being built or barely held. A deficiency that climbs to 86% in children and 62% in pregnancy is not a footnote to a clean diet; it is the diet's structural debt, paid by *majjā* (marrow and nervous tissue) and by the unborn. The neuropsychiatric face of that debt — slowed cognition, memory failure, depression, and in the severe case frank psychosis reversible on repletion — is documented across a wide case literature and is the verified mechanism behind the kind of breakdown the diet articles describe anecdotally.

## IV. Infants and children — the gravest harm

The clearest demonstrations of harm are the ones no one would design: children placed on strict raw or fruit diets by their parents, with no capacity to seek other food. The courts and the coroners have produced the record.

The strongest case in which causation was adjudicated is the 2019 death of an eighteen-month-old boy, Ezra O'Leary, in Florida. The family ate only raw fruit and vegetables; the child weighed seventeen pounds at death — the size of a seven-month-old — and three surviving siblings were found malnourished and dehydrated. The mother was convicted of first-degree murder and aggravated child abuse and sentenced to life in 2022. The case is reported in the mainstream record (NBC News; *Global News*, 2019–2022). Causation here was not a clinician's inference but a jury's finding.

The forensic literature carries a second case, which must be cited with care because its authors dispute the cause of death:

> "The infant, fed an exclusively raw foods vegan formula of blended nuts, seeds, vegetables, and coconut water after 2½ months of breastfeeding, weighed 3,180 g (6.99 lb) at autopsy and appeared emaciated; the thymus gland was absent and the parathyroid glands were not located. Four older siblings on the same diet fell 2.1 to 4.1 standard deviations below the growth-chart means for height and weight."\
> — D. K. Cundiff, W. Harris, "Case Report of 5 Siblings: Malnutrition? Rickets? DiGeorge Syndrome? Developmental Delay?," *Nutrition Journal* 2006;5:1

The autopsy facts are unambiguous: an emaciated infant, a vanished thymus, a quartet of siblings stunted by two to four standard deviations. The authors themselves — one a vegan advocate — argue the coroner could not confirm malnutrition by WHO criteria and attribute part of the siblings' small stature to inherited build. Cited honestly, the paper documents what an exclusively raw vegan diet did to five children's bodies while declining to call it the cause; the article that uses it should report the facts and the dispute together, not the facts alone. Both cases say the same thing the *Taittirīya Upaniṣad* says from the other direction: the body is *anna-rasa-maya*, built from the essence of cooked grain (Tait. Up. 2.1), and a growing body denied that substance does not grow.

## V. The fruitarian anecdotes — what they show and what they do not

The diet articles carry three modern anecdotes. Verification sorts them into the citable, the cautionary, and the unsupported.

**Citable as self-report.** Ashton Kutcher, preparing to play Steve Jobs for the 2013 film, adopted Jobs' fruitarian diet and was hospitalized two days before filming with his "pancreas levels completely out of whack" — pancreatitis, by his own account (*The Hollywood Reporter*, Jan. 2013; CNBC, 2019). This is a celebrity's first-person account, not a clinical study, and carries exactly that weight: one vivid instance of the sugar-flood the fruitarian plate delivers, reported by the man it happened to.

**Unsupported as stated.** The claim that Steve Jobs "died of pancreatic cancer — disease in the organ most burdened by the diet" implies a causal link the evidence does not carry. Jobs died of a pancreatic neuroendocrine (islet-cell) tumor, a rare cancer with no established dietary cause and no demonstrated tie to fruit or sugar intake. The biographical facts are usable — Jobs was the era's most famous fruitarian, and Kutcher's scare while imitating him is a genuine irony — but the organ-burdened-by-the-diet causation should be dropped. Naming a real harm the diet does not demonstrably cause weakens the many harms it demonstrably does.

**Real mechanism, unlocated instance.** The image of a fruitarian admitted to a psychiatric ward with delusions and memory collapse, recovered on returning to ordinary food, was not located as a single citable case. The mechanism behind it is nonetheless well attested: B-12 and thiamine deficiency on restricted vegetal diets produces psychosis, confusion, and memory failure that reverse on repletion, documented across a substantial case literature (see §III). The article should ground the claim in that literature rather than in an unsourced individual, or drop the individual and keep the mechanism.

## VI. The shape of the evidence

The clinical record and the classical corpus describe one process in two vocabularies. The raw, fruit-dominant diet clears — it lowers the lipids the Giessen lipid study found favorable, it lifts the early heaviness every account reports — and then, sustained, it depletes: weight to underweight, menstruation to amenorrhea, B-12 to deficiency, enamel to erosion, bone to low mass, and, in the child who cannot choose otherwise, growth to stunting and worse. Every one of these is the *vāta*-aggravating, *dhātu*-wasting, *ojas*-draining trajectory the tradition assigned to a diet built on the clearing principle without the binding one. The modern studies did not discover the pattern. They measured it.
