# Raw Vegan, Salad, and Juice — the Classical Verdict on the Modern Raw Modes

An *applied* digest. Three modern eating-modes claim the mantle of purity and yogic lightness: the **raw salad** (large quantities of raw leafy greens and vegetables), the **raw-vegan diet** (the whole plate uncooked), and the **juice cleanse** (fruit and green juice as staple or fast). This file assembles what the Ayurvedic and Yogic corpora say each of these does to the body, and why yoga — far from condoning them — excludes them by name.

The verse-level evidence for the underlying doctrine lives in three companion files; this digest cites them rather than re-quoting in full:

- The cooking/āma doctrine and the *anna-rasa-prāṇa* chain: [`Cooking_and_Agni.md`](./Cooking_and_Agni.md).
- The *mitāhāra* / *apathya* / *viruddha* / rasa-atiyoga verses: [`Apathya.md`](./Apathya.md).
- The cross-tradition witnesses (Hippocrates, Galen, Avicenna, Chinese medicine, Lévi-Strauss, Wrangham — including the Giessen raw-food clinical study): [`Raw_and_Cooked_Cross_Tradition.md`](./Raw_and_Cooked_Cross_Tradition.md).

This compilation supports [*Yogis Don't Eat Fruit*](../diet/Yogis_Dont_Eat_Fruit.md) and any future article on raw-veganism, salad, or juicing.

## The governing law — like increases like

The engine under all three verdicts is the first principle of Ayurvedic pharmacology, the *sāmānya-viśeṣa* doctrine: likeness increases, unlikeness decreases (*Caraka Saṃhitā*, Sūtrasthāna 1.44–45). A substance builds in the body whatever quality it carries. The raw plate carries *rūkṣa* (dry), *śīta* (cold), *laghu* (light), *khara* (rough), and *viśada* (scouring) — and these are precisely the qualities of *vāta*. A diet of them raises *vāta* by the same law that a diet of warm, unctuous, heavy grain-and-ghee settles it. Everything below is this law applied.

## I. The salad — large quantities of raw greens

The classical corpus never contemplates raw leafy greens as a staple. The instruction for heavy vegetables is explicit and is the exact inverse of the salad: *"boiled, the juice expressed out, and added with fat before intake"* (Caraka Sū. 27, the *śāka-varga* preparation rule — see [`../caraka-samhita/fruit-vegetable-warnings.md`](https://github.com/LiGoldragon/caraka-samhita/blob/main/fruit-vegetable-warnings.md)). Boil, press, butter — not raw, cold, and dry.

The consequence of the dry-rough-astringent quality in excess is stated directly in the rasa-atiyoga verses (full Sanskrit in [`Apathya.md`](./Apathya.md) §IV):

> "by its dryness and its rough, scouring nature, [it] dries up the plasma, blood, flesh, fat, bone, marrow, and semen; roughens the channels; takes away strength; emaciates; and gives rise to the disorders of *vāta*."\
> — *Caraka Saṃhitā*, Sūtrasthāna 26.43 (*tikta* in excess)

> "[it] dries the mouth, distends the abdomen, blocks the channels, produces discoloration; digests with obstruction, and withholds the downward flow of *vāta*, urine, faeces, and semen."\
> — *Caraka Saṃhitā*, Sūtrasthāna 26.43 (*kaṣāya* in excess)

Raw leaves are *kaṣāya* (astringent) and *tikta* (bitter); eaten in volume they produce the constipation, distension, channel-obstruction, dryness, and *vāta* derangement the verses name. The Caraka vegetable catalogue (Sū. 27.88–124) compounds the warning: many greens are *guru* (heavy), *śīta* (cold), and *srotorodhin* (channel-blocking) even before the rawness is counted (digest: [`../caraka-samhita/fruit-vegetable-warnings.md`](https://github.com/LiGoldragon/caraka-samhita/blob/main/fruit-vegetable-warnings.md)).

## II. The juice — fruit and green juice as staple or fast

Juice concentrates the error of fruit and strips the only mitigations chewing leaves intact. Three classical objections converge:

1. **Single-rasa excess.** Juice is one *rasa* — *madhura* (sweet) for fruit, *tikta/kaṣāya* for green — taken in volume. Caraka's daily-eating criterion is the consumption of *all six rasas*; the chronic intake of *one* is debilitating: *"regular intake of all the rasas among the strength-promoting ones, regular use of one rasa among the debilitating ones"* (Sū. 25.40; full block in [`Fruit_as_Medicine.md`](./Fruit_as_Medicine.md) §IV). Sweet alone, in excess, *"causes obesity, softness, laziness, excessive sleep, heaviness, loss of appetite, weakness of agni"* (madhura-atiyoga, Sū. 26.43).
2. **Cold dousing agni.** Juice is *śīta* (cold) and is drunk, not eaten — and cold drink before or with food is the classical way to weaken the digestive fire. The agni-kindling food is *uṣṇa* (warm); *"all diseases arise where agni is weak"* (*rogāḥ sarve'pi mandāgnau*, after AH Sū. 12; see [`Cooking_and_Agni.md`](./Cooking_and_Agni.md) §III).
3. **The chewing and the fibre removed.** Juicing delivers the dispersing, cold essence with the fibre and the mastication stripped — the solar, clearing principle distilled, with nothing left to slow or ground it.

## III. The raw-vegan whole plate — āma and the depletion arc

When the entire plate is uncooked, the cooking that should have begun in the kitchen is demanded of the belly. The canonical anchor: *na hy apakvād rasādayaḥ* — *"from uncooked food, rasa and the rest do not arise"* (*Aṣṭāṅga Hṛdaya* Śārīrasthāna 3.54; full block in [`Cooking_and_Agni.md`](./Cooking_and_Agni.md) §II). What the belly's fire cannot finish becomes *āma* — undigested residue, the material basis of disease (Caraka Sū. 25.40; Suśruta Sū. 19).

The clinical arc the texts predict — *ojas*-depletion, *śukra*-loss, *vāta* derangement, tissue-wasting — is documented in the modern record by the Giessen Raw Food study: among women on a fully raw diet, about half ceased to menstruate, and a third showed chronic energy deficiency (Koebnick et al., via Wrangham — see [`Raw_and_Cooked_Cross_Tradition.md`](./Raw_and_Cooked_Cross_Tradition.md) §V). The Sanskrit register names the same outcome *śukra-kṣaya* and *vandhyatva* — depletion of generative essence, infertility. Hippocrates names the raw-fed body's fate as wasting, weakness, and inability "to survive for very long" (*Ancient Medicine* 13).

## IV. Why yoga does not condone it

The yogic plate is positively specified, and the specification excludes the raw modes on every axis:

- **Yoga requires *mitāhāra* — measured eating — or it fails.** *"Without moderate diet, whoever begins yoga practice will acquire various diseases and will not succeed in yoga"* (Gheraṇḍa Saṃhitā 5.16). The Gītā states the same in the positive: yoga belongs to the *yuktāhāra* and is barred to the one who eats too much or fasts to depletion (BhG 6.16–17). The juice-faster and the raw-gorger occupy the two excluded poles. (Both blocks: [`Apathya.md`](./Apathya.md) §II.)
- **The yogic food is cooked, unctuous, sweet.** *Annaṁ yogārthinā sadā* — the yogin always eats *anna*, cooked grain (proverbial yogic maxim); the meal is *snigdha-madhura*, unctuous and sweet (HYP 1.58), *"mixed with ghee and milk"* (HYP 1.63); the Gītā's sāttvic food is *snigdha* and *sthira*, unctuous and stable (BhG 17.8). Raw salad is *rūkṣa* (dry) and *cala* (mobile) — the inverse of *snigdha-sthira*.
- **Yoga names raw greens in the prohibition list.** The *apathya* verse opens with *harita-śāka* — fresh/raw greens (HYP 1.59); the *Gheraṇḍa Saṃhitā* forbids *"an excess of leafy greens"* (GhS 5.21). (Blocks: [`Apathya.md`](./Apathya.md) §III, [`Cooking_and_Agni.md`](./Cooking_and_Agni.md) §VI.)

The logic closes: yoga depends on a kindled *agni* and a settled *vāta*. The raw salad, the raw-vegan plate, and the juice cleanse each weaken *agni* and raise *vāta*. A practice cannot be built on the food that dismantles its own preconditions. The raw regimen is the fruitarian error in a second key — the solar, dispersing principle without the lunar, gathering one.

## Synthesis

1. **The law:** like increases like (Caraka Sū. 1.44–45). The raw plate's qualities are *vāta*'s qualities; it raises *vāta*.
2. **The salad:** raw greens are *tikta-kaṣāya*, *rūkṣa*, *śīta*; in volume they dry the *dhātus*, roughen and block the channels, and derange *vāta* (Caraka Sū. 26.43; 27.88–124). The text's instruction for greens is boil-press-butter, not raw.
3. **The juice:** single-rasa excess (Sū. 25.40), cold dousing *agni* (AH Sū. 12), chewing and fibre stripped — the dispersing essence distilled.
4. **The raw-vegan plate:** *na hy apakvād rasādayaḥ* — uncooked food yields *āma*, not *rasa* (AH Śā. 3.54); the arc ends in *ojas*- and *śukra*-depletion, documented clinically (Giessen study).
5. **Yoga's verdict:** *mitāhāra* of cooked *snigdha-sthira anna* is required (Gheraṇḍa Saṃhitā 5.16; HYP 1.58, 1.63; BhG 6.16–17, 17.8); raw greens are named *apathya* (HYP 1.59; GhS 5.21). Yoga does not condone the raw modes; it forbids them and prescribes their opposite.
