The ghee on the shelf did not appear from nowhere. A cow produced it. She stood somewhere -- perhaps in a feedlot, perhaps on a hillside -- and her body made milk, and the milk was churned, and the butter was clarified, and the jar was sealed and shelved under fluorescent light. But she is in it. The substance carries her.

The jar is not a commodity. It is a letter from a mother you have never met, written in the only language her body knows.

Someone will buy that jar. If not you, then the next person -- who may dump it on steak without thought and never consider what was given or who gave it. But when you take the jar knowing what it is, knowing who made it -- the link activates. Appreciation is not a feeling. It is a force.

She fights by giving. A cow does not withhold nourishment out of spite. She gives as a mother gives: unconditionally, because giving is what she is.

You fight by receiving. You take the substance and recognize it. You grow stronger not despite the link but through it. Your side of the war is fought with tapas -- the quiet heat of a person who has decided that this exchange is not a purchase but a covenant.

Every jar received with consciousness is a battle fought for our common freedom -- where she is free to live a life worthy of her gentleness, and I am free to walk onto the land, kneel down, and wrap my arms around her head.

They are my mothers. They are my sisters in spirit. We are besties.

I am glad I ate the Walmart butter. It made me a more formidable warrior. It fed me and it woke me up. The ojas built strength. The awareness built duty. It is my duty to create a domain for them.

So for these last months I have been working harder, with more joy than at any point in my life. Every hour of labor is a brick in a wall that will enclose a pasture. And the cows know. They are already standing in the field that does not yet exist, because the field exists in intention, and intention is the first material of creation.

Soon is not a wish. It is a schedule.
